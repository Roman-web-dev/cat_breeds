Abyssinian cat

Description

The abyssinian cat breed originates from Ethiopia. Its scientific name is 'Felis catus'. The breed is described as curious, sensible, loyal, intelligent, willful and extroverted.

Best for:

  • Active Families: Abyssinians thrive in active households. If you’re a family that enjoys playtime, interactive games, and climbing adventures, the Aby will happily join the fun. Their playful nature makes them ideal companions for energetic kids.
  • Social Butterflies: Abyssinians are the life of the party! They love people and other animals. If you’re a social person who enjoys having a lively and interactive pet, the Aby will fit right in. They’ll chat with you in their soft, quiet voice and seek attention from everyone in the household.
  • Multi-Pet Homes: Whether you already have a dog or another cat, the Abyssinian will likely get along splendidly. They’re social creatures and appreciate having company. So, if you’re part of a furry family, consider adding an Aby to the mix—they’ll keep everyone entertained!
  • Adventurous Souls: Abyssinians are curious explorers. They’ll investigate every nook and cranny, race up and down stairs, and even try to grab things off counters (hence their nickname “Aby-grabbys”). If you’re an adventurous spirit who enjoys a cat with a zest for life, the Aby is your match.
  • Cat Lovers Who Want a Unique Look: With their elegant, ticked coat patterns (available in ruddy, red, blue, cinnamon, or fawn), Abyssinians are visually striking. If you appreciate a cat that stands out in a crowd, the Aby’s refined appearance will capture your heart.
